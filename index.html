Immersive AR History Experience


    The Problem:
    • People have no incentives to visit historical sites
    • People visit historical sites but don't learn much about the significance and the history behind them

    The Solution:
    • Find an interactive and fun way to visualise and the understand the history of a place when you're there

    How:
    • Working with our mentors at PBS WI
    • Create an Augmented Reality Experience to immerse the user into the location
    • MindAR framework for the AR and React for the Website

    Why:
    • From our interviews, we understood that people would want to visit historical sites if there was a way to visualize the history at the location better.

    The project was created by a team of 4 members, each working as a Software Engineer but also picking up more responsibilities in an Agile environment. While Mulan and Ankit focused on the AR aspect of the project, Vrishank picked up the responsibilities of managing the frontend of the project and I picked up the responsibilities of the Project Manager and created a timeline for the project to be completed in about 3 months (as a semester long project). By the end of the 3 month period, we not only completed the project from the programming point of view, but I also created some 3D models to make a surprising and fun in-person final demonstration of the project instead of only using assets that we had found online for free use.




    Mendify - Mental Health made easier


    We wanted to create this application to better meet the unique needs of college students, focusing on tools for emotional regulation, mindfulness, and easier/natural access to mental health resources at the university. Our team's goal was to implement an app that combines journaling, therapist/provider scheduling, guided practices, and crisis support in a single, accessible platform to achieve these goals.

    Guided by the findings of our Contextual Interviews, we developed core design concepts to address the aforementioned challenges. Our main ideas include:

    1. A unified dashboard that consolidates all mental health resources
    2. An emergency functionality that can be reached in less than 2 clicks
    3. A clear therapist scheduling feature that humanizes and segregates the process between routine and crisis
    4. A secure journaling tool



    QuickBite - A Food App Solution


    For this final project, me and my group interviewed multiple people about their experience with food delivery apps. After a set of interviews and noting down user needs and pain points the main problem that we arrived at was “How might we create a reliable way to sort through open/closed restaurants and narrow down the choices for food in a food delivery app?”

    Following the brainstorming, our team landed on three main ideas to pursue for our final design which were intuitive tiles, user profiles, and filters based on time and mood.

    1. The goal of intuitive tiles was to have the tiles clearly show the user whether the restaurant was open, closing soon, or was closed. Our idea was to represent these two ways. The first was to have the restaurant's background either be green, yellow, or red to demonstrate its status. The second way was to have the restaurant's information dimmed based on its status. We decided to add both ways to make the app accessible for color-blind users who might not be able to tell the difference between the colors.

    2. Our second idea was to have user profiles where users could set preferences such as favorite foods and dietary restrictions. If a user was vegetarian, they could set that dietary restriction in their preferences and then would not be shown food with meat in it unless requested.

    3. Our third idea was to have filters based on time and mood. The goal behind this idea was to have filters that a user could select that would show them popular choices based on the time of their order or what mood they are feeling. It would also show the users' favorite/most commonly ordered choices around the current time.
